## LinkedList

- Instead of storing elements next to each other in memory - Arrays

    [Truth][Mary][John][David]

- We sort each element seperately and connect them with links - LinkedList

    [Truth | • ] ──► [Mary | • ] ──► [John | • ] ──► [David | None]

        . Each box is called a Node;
            Node
            ├── Data
            └── Next

            
e.g:
    Node
    ├── "Truth"
    └── address of Mary

# Advantage
- Inserting, and Deleting(Modifying at the beginning or end) cost less - O(1)
- No shifting

# Disadvantage
- Accessing cost more - O(n)
- Does not use index hence we have to work      through the chain
